IEEE TENSYMP 2027

Region 10 Symposium

Connected Intelligence for Resilient Societies

12–14 May 2027
Faculty of Engineering, University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka

Organized By

About IEEE Sri Lanka Section

The IEEE Sri Lanka Section represents a vibrant and growing community of engineers, researchers, academics, and students committed to advancing technology for the benefit of humanity. Since its inauguration in 2003, the Section has played a key role in strengthening Sri Lanka’s presence within the global IEEE ecosystem through technical excellence, leadership, and impactful collaborations.

About IEEE TENSYMP 2027

IEEE TENSYMP 2027, to be held from 12–14 May 2027 at the Faculty of Engineering, University of Peradeniya, marks a significant milestone for the IEEE Sri Lanka Section and the wider IEEE Asia–Pacific community. Under the theme “Connected Intelligence for Resilient Societies”, the symposium provides a high-impact platform for advancing interdisciplinary research, innovation, and collaboration at the intersection of intelligent technologies and societal resilience.

Conference Theme & Vision

This theme highlights the integration of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, communications and the Internet of Things, and intelligent sensing and actuation technologies to strengthen resilient infrastructures across the Asia–Pacific region. It reflects the collaborative focus of IEEE technical societies—including the IEEE Computer Society, IEEE Signal Processing Society, IEEE Communications Society, IEEE Sensors Council, and IEEE Robotics and Automation Society—toward addressing critical challenges such as climate change, natural disasters, and global health crises. Aligned with IEEE’s mission of “Advancing Technology for Humanity”, the theme underscores how connected intelligence can empower societies to anticipate, withstand, and adapt to emerging disruptions.

IEEE Collaboration & Global Impact

The theme Connected Intelligence for Resilient Societies directly supports the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, particularly SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure), SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities), and SDG 13 (Climate Action). Through multidisciplinary collaboration among IEEE communities such as the IEEE Computer Society, IEEE Communications Society, and IEEE Sensors Council, the theme promotes intelligent, connected systems that enhance disaster preparedness, climate resilience, smart infrastructure, and digital health. Aligned with IEEE’s mission of Advancing Technology for Humanity, it emphasizes ethical, inclusive, and sustainable innovation to strengthen societal resilience across the Asia–Pacific region.

Proposed Technical Tracks

Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Data Intelligence

AI for social good, resilient systems, predictive analytics, and intelligent decision-making frameworks

Future Communication & Networking Technologies

5G/6G networks, IoT infrastructure, sensor platforms, and secure communications for resilient connectivity.

Cyber-Physical Systems, IoT, and Intelligent Automation

Smart sensing, real-time control, robotics, and integrated CPS for sustainable and adaptive applications.

Sustainable Energy & Environmental Technologies

Renewable energy systems, smart grids, environmental sensing and climate-resilient designs strategies.

Cybersecurity Privacy, and Resilient Computing

Secure architectures, privacy-preserving systems, fault-tolerant computing, and trust in connected systems.

Technology for Health ,Humanity & Society

AI-powered healthcare, telemedicine, and ICT for underserved communities and trust in connected systems.

Ethics, Policy, and Governance in Connected Systems

Responsible AI, data governance, standards, and ethical challenges in deploying connected intelligence.

Education and Capacity Building in Resilient Technologies

Digital literacy, workforce upskilling, and engineering education for sustainable futures.
