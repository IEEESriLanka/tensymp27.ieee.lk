IEEE TENSYMP 2027

Region 10 Symposium

Connected Intelligence for Resilient Societies

12–14 May 2027
Faculty of Engineering, University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka

About IEEE Sri Lanka Section

The IEEE Sri Lanka Section represents a vibrant and growing community of engineers, researchers, academics, and students committed to advancing technology for the benefit of humanity. Since its inauguration in 2003, the Section has played a key role in strengthening Sri Lanka’s presence within the global IEEE ecosystem through technical excellence, leadership, and impactful collaborations.

About IEEE TENSYMP 2027

IEEE TENSYMP 2027, to be held from 12–14 May 2027 at the Faculty of Engineering, University of Peradeniya, marks a significant milestone for the IEEE Sri Lanka Section and the wider IEEE Asia–Pacific community. Under the theme “Connected Intelligence for Resilient Societies”, the symposium provides a high-impact platform for advancing interdisciplinary research, innovation, and collaboration at the intersection of intelligent technologies and societal resilience.

Conference Theme & Vision

The theme “Connected Intelligence for Resilient Societies” explores how the convergence of Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, the Internet of Things, advanced sensing and actuation, robotics, and next-generation communication technologies can enable societies to become more adaptive, secure, and sustainable.

In a region increasingly affected by climate change, natural disasters, healthcare inequities, and digital divides, TENSYMP 2027 focuses on technologies that allow communities not only to respond to disruptions but also to anticipate and recover effectively.

IEEE Collaboration & Global Impact

This vision aligns strongly with IEEE’s leading technical communities, including the IEEE Computer Society, IEEE Signal Processing Society, IEEE Communications Society, IEEE Sensors Council, and the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society. Together, these communities bring complementary expertise to address complex real-world challenges through integrated and connected intelligence.

In line with IEEE’s mission of “Advancing Technology for Humanity” and the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, TENSYMP 2027 highlights technologies contributing to Climate Action (SDG 13), Resilient Infrastructure (SDG 9), Sustainable Cities (SDG 11), Good Health and Well-Being (SDG 3), and Affordable and Clean Energy (SDG 7).

Proposed Technical Tracks

Artificial Intelligence & Data Intelligence

AI for social good, predictive analytics, and intelligent decision-making.

Future Communication & Networking

5G/6G, IoT infrastructures, and secure, resilient connectivity.

Cyber-Physical Systems & Intelligent Automation

Smart sensing, robotics, and real-time control systems.

Sustainable Energy & Environmental Technologies

Renewable energy, smart grids, and climate-resilient designs.

Cybersecurity & Resilient Computing

Secure architectures, privacy, fault tolerance, and trusted systems.

Technology for Health & Society

AI-driven healthcare, telemedicine, and ICT for underserved communities.

Ethics, Policy & Governance

Responsible AI, data governance, standards, and regulation.

Education & Capacity Building

Digital literacy, workforce upskilling, and future-ready education.
