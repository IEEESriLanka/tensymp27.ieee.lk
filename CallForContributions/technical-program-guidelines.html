Technical Program Guidelines

Home > Call for Contributions > Technical Program Guidelines

The technical program committee is responsible for ensuring the high quality of the conference content. The following guidelines outline the structure and expectations for the technical program.

  1. Scope and Tracks

    The conference is organized into several technical tracks. Authors should select the most appropriate track for their submission to ensure it is reviewed by experts in the field. The main tracks include:

    • Track 1: Algorithms and Complexity
    • Track 2: Network Architectures and Protocols
    • Track 3: Signal Processing
    • Track 4: Industrial Applications

  2. Review Process

    All submissions will undergo a double-blind peer review process. At least three independent reviewers will evaluate each paper based on:

    • Technical Novelty and Innovation
    • Relevance to the Conference Scope
    • Clarity of Presentation
    • Validity of Experimental Results

  3. Ethical Standards

    Authors must adhere to strict ethical standards. Plagiarism, including self-plagiarism, is strictly prohibited. All papers must be original and not simultaneously submitted to another journal or conference.

  4. Conflicts of Interest

    Reviewers and committee members must declare any conflicts of interest with submitted papers. Papers where a conflict exists will be reassigned to ensure an impartial review.
